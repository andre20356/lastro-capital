CHARGE MODALITIES RULES
--------------------------------------------------

- The due date is the current charge due date.

  - Due date = previous due date + 1 day
  - Status = pending
- Do NOT generate multiple future charges in advance.
- Always generate only the NEXT charge after payment.

2) WEEKLY CHARGE ("semanal"):
- The charge value represents a WEEKLY installment amount.
- When a weekly charge is marked as PAID:
  - Automatically generate a new charge for the SAME CLIENT
  - Same installment value and same description

--------------------------------------------------
INTEREST (JUROS) LOGIC – DAILY AND WEEKLY ONLY
--------------------------------------------------

- DAILY and WEEKLY charges represent installment-based contracts.
- The user provides:
  - Principal amount (borrowed value)
  - Installment value
  - Number of installments
- The app must automatically calculate:
  - Total amount to be paid
  - Total interest percentage of the contract

Interest percentage formula:
((installmentValue × numberOfInstallments - principalAmount) / principalAmount) × 100

RULES:
- Interest is calculated for the FULL CONTRACT, not per day or per week.
- The calculated interest percentage must be read-only (auto-calculated).
- Do NOT apply interest calculation to monthly charges.

LATE FEE (TAXA DE ATRASO) LOGIC
--------------------------------------------------

- DAILY and WEEKLY contracts must include a configurable late fee rate.

Required new field (only if not existing):

RULES:
- Late fee is applied ONLY when a charge is overdue.
- Late fee affects ONLY the overdue installment.
- Late fee must NOT:
  - Generate new charges
  - Modify future installments
  - Modify the original contract values

Late fee calculation:
- DAILY: late fee applied per DAY of delay
- WEEKLY: late fee applied per WEEK of delay

Examples:
- Weekly installment: 350
- Late fee rate: 10% per week
- 1 week late → 350 + 10% = 385

- Daily installment: 50
- Late fee rate: 2% per day
- 3 days late → 50 + (2% × 3)

The updated amount should be calculated dynamically based on delay time.
